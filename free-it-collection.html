E Waste Collection

Understanding the 10-item minimum rule, eligible business hardware, and vehicle transport logistics.

Last Updated: 29th June 2026

What is an E-Waste Collection? Definition and Examples

An e-waste collection is the physical removal and transport of obsolete electrical items for compliant recycling. Examples of collected items include laptops, computer monitors, office printers, and network switches.

Clearcycle IT operates a B2B collection infrastructure across the UK, helping corporate offices, educational establishments, and public sector sites safely decommission and dispose of redundant electrical hardware.

Which 5 major hardware units qualify for free B2B collection?

Clearcycle IT structures collection eligibility around the volume of high-value computer assets. We require a minimum of 10 major IT units to deploy a transport vehicle.

Five categories of equipment qualify directly as major IT units: desktop PCs, laptop computers, enterprise servers, computer monitors, and large office photocopiers.

Why are 3 main environmental licensing rules crucial for business collections?

UK businesses face strict statutory liabilities regarding e-waste disposal. Under Section 34 of the Environmental Protection Act 1990, three rules define compliance for e-waste collection:

  1. Upper Tier Carrier Registration: The transport provider must hold an active upper tier waste carrier licence on the Environment Agency register. Clearcycle IT holds registration CBDU646177. Attending drivers carry a printed copy of this certificate to confirm legal transport authorization.
  2. Waste Transfer Note Manifests: Every transport load requires a signed Waste Transfer Note (WTN) on handover. This document records the description, volume, and EWC codes of all items loaded. Clearcycle IT pre-populates this paperwork from your booking data to speed up the collection process.
  3. Hazardous Waste Consignment Notes: Equipment containing hazardous components (such as mercury backlights in LCD screens, fluorescent lamps, or nickel-cadmium batteries) requires a specialized consignment note instead of a standard WTN. This ensures that the environmental agency can trace the transport of toxic chemicals to authorised treatment locations.

To prevent compliance errors, Clearcycle IT filters out domestic e-waste during the booking process. We do not accept household appliances or consumer electronics. Examples of non-accepted consumer items include domestic refrigerators, microwaves, washing machines, and personal kitchen appliances.

By focusing exclusively on B2B IT hardware, our vehicles operate within optimized payload limits. This reduces unnecessary transport loops and lowers fuel usage, helping our clients minimize their scope 3 environmental impact.

How do accessories integrate with the 10-item minimum threshold?

Peripherals and networking accessories are excluded from the 10-item minimum calculation. This rule filters out single-item consumer requests. However, once an organisation qualifies for collection by meeting the 10-major-unit threshold, our transport crews collect all accompanying IT accessories at no additional cost.

This comprehensive collection policy simplifies office clearances. Clearcycle IT accepts cables, keyboards, mice, power leads, and desk phones alongside qualifying PCs and servers. This ensures a complete site clearance without the need to arrange secondary collections.

What is the step-by-step dispatch and transport safety process?

Clearcycle IT operates a fleet of secure transport vehicles. Our collection logistics follow a structured 4-step transport safety workflow:

  1. Step 1: Manifest Alignment: Before loading, the driver verifies the quantities against the booking details.
  2. Step 2: Secure Packaging: High-value assets are packed into sealed plastic crates, preventing damage and theft.
  3. Step 3: Tracked Transit: The vehicle travels via GPS-monitored routes directly to our Manchester recycling facility.
  4. Step 4: Receipt Logging: Receiving engineers weigh the payload and scan all items into our inventory database.

How does secure collection protect corporate data?

Transporting active storage media presents a chain-of-custody risk. Under the UK GDPR, data controllers are responsible for protecting data-bearing drives during transit. Clearcycle IT minimizes this risk by deploying directly-employed, DBS-checked drivers and using sealed transit bins.

Once the vehicle arrives at our Manchester processing facility, all hard drives and SSDs undergo certified sanitisation under strict security protocols. We issue full Certificates of Destruction and WEEE waste transfer documentation to close the audit loop. This comprehensive record package ensures total data safety and environmental compliance for B2B waste audits.
