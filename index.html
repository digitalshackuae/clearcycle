OUR SECURITY PROMISE

Your Data Never Leaves Our Process

Security is our core priority. From collection to destruction, we maintain a secure chain of custody that exceeds standard government requirements.

STEP 01

Tracked Vehicles

Our logistics team collects directly from your site using tracked, secure vehicles. The chain of custody begins the moment we lift the assets.

STEP 02

DBS-Checked Staff

All members of our collection and destruction teams undergo background checks, providing schools and institutions complete peace of mind.

STEP 03

Data Sanitisation

We use Blancco-level secure software to erase every sector on drives, aligning with the HMG Infosec Standard 5 for data destruction.

STEP 04

Physical Destruction

Any hard drive or SSD that fails software erasure is physically shredded, rendering data retrieval completely impossible.

STEP 05

Certificates Issued

You receive a formal Certificate of Destruction listing the serial numbers of all wiped and shredded hard drives for your audit trail.

STEP 06

Compliance Reports

A full environmental and data compliance report is provided, proving you have met WEEE and GDPR legal obligations.

Do You Qualify for a Free Collection?

We collect free of charge from UK businesses, schools, and colleges when they meet our minimum major hardware threshold.

We Collect

  • Laptops & MacBooks

    Any age, brand, or specification. Collected with or without power adaptors.

  • Desktop PCs & iMacs

    Tower units, all-in-ones, micro-form factors and workstations.

  • Servers & Storage

    Server racks, SAN storage networks, mainframes and network cabinets.

  • Office Monitors

    LCD/LED flatscreens, desktop monitor stands and multi-display arms.

We Don't Collect

  • Household Computers

    Residential computer recycling or consumer drop-offs are not supported.

  • Single Desktop PC/Laptop

    We do not arrange collections for individual devices. 10-item minimum applies.

  • Small Domestic Collections

    Not suitable for personal or domestic use. Strictly commercial/educational.

  • Home Entertainment

    Games consoles, televisions, VHS players and residential sound systems.

  • Household Appliances

    Washing machines, fridges, microwaves or small residential kitchen items.
